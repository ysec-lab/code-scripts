## 1. MALWARE THREATS — Malicious software designed to infiltrate, damage, or steal.

### Core Malware
* **Virus** — Infects files and spreads when those files execute.
* **Worm** — Self‑replicates across networks without user action.
* **Trojan** — Disguises itself as legitimate software to gain access.
* **Ransomware** — Encrypts or locks data until ransom is paid.
* **Spyware** — Secretly monitors user activity and steals information.
* **Adware** — Displays unwanted ads and may track behavior.
* **Keylogger** — Records keystrokes to steal credentials.
* **Botnet** — Network of infected devices controlled remotely.
* **Fileless malware** — Operates in memory to evade detection.
* **Wiper** — Permanently destroys data or systems.

### Advanced Malware
* **RAT (Remote Access Trojan)** — Provides full remote control over an infected system.
* **Rootkit** — Hides malicious activity by modifying OS components.
* **Bootkit** — Infects the bootloader/UEFI to gain pre‑OS persistence.
* **Loader** — Downloads and executes additional malware payloads.
* **Dropper** — Installs or “drops” malware onto a system.
* **Packer** — Obfuscates malware by compressing/encrypting code.
* **Infostealer** — Steals credentials, browser data, crypto wallets.
* **Backdoor** — Provides persistent unauthorized remote access.
* **Polymorphic malware** — Constantly changes code to evade detection.
* **Metamorphic malware** — Rewrites its own code entirely on each infection.
* **Banking Trojan** — Steals financial data or manipulates transactions.

---

## 2. PHISHING & SOCIAL ENGINEERING — Attacks targeting human behavior.

* **Phishing** — Mass fraudulent messages to steal data.
* **Spear phishing** — Targeted phishing aimed at specific individuals.
* **Whaling** — Phishing targeting executives.
* **Smishing** — SMS‑based phishing.
* **Vishing** — Voice‑based phishing calls.
* **Clone phishing** — Copies legitimate emails with malicious links.
* **Business Email Compromise** — Impersonates executives to steal money or data.
* **Pretexting** — Fabricated scenarios to extract information.
* **Baiting** — Lures victims with false incentives.
* **Quid pro quo** — Offers something in exchange for access or info.

---

## 3. IDENTITY & ACCESS ATTACKS — Target authentication and authorization.

* **Credential stuffing** — Using leaked passwords across many accounts.
* **Password spraying** — Trying common passwords on many accounts.
* **Brute force attack** — Systematically guessing passwords.
* **Token theft** — Stealing authentication tokens to bypass login.
* **Session hijacking** — Taking over active user sessions.
* **AiTM (Adversary‑in‑the‑Middle)** — Intercepts authentication flows.
* **Privilege escalation** — Gaining higher‑level access than intended.

---

## 4. NETWORK & INFRASTRUCTURE ATTACKS — Target routers, firewalls, servers, and protocols.

* **DDoS** — Overwhelms systems to cause outages.
* **Man‑in‑the‑Middle** — Intercepts communications.
* **DNS poisoning** — Redirects traffic to malicious sites.
* **BGP hijacking** — Manipulates internet routing paths.
* **ARP spoofing** — Impersonates devices on a local network.
* **Port scanning** — Probes systems for open ports.
* **SMB exploitation** — Attacks SMB protocol vulnerabilities.
* **VPN exploitation** — Compromises VPN software or credentials.

---

## 5. APPLICATION & SOFTWARE EXPLOITS — Exploit vulnerabilities in code.

* **SQL injection** — Injects malicious queries into databases.
* **XSS** — Injects malicious scripts into web pages.
* **CSRF** — Forces users to perform unintended actions.
* **Remote code execution** — Executes arbitrary code on a target system.
* **Zero‑day exploit** — Exploits unknown vulnerabilities.
* **Memory corruption** — Exploits buffer overflows or similar flaws.
* **API abuse** — Misuses or attacks APIs.

---

## 6. CLOUD SECURITY THREATS — Target cloud platforms and SaaS.

* **Misconfigured storage buckets** — Exposes cloud data publicly.
* **IAM misconfigurations** — Excessive permissions or weak identity controls.
* **Container escape** — Breaking out of container isolation.
* **Supply chain compromise** — Attacking third‑party software/services.
* **Shadow IT** — Unauthorized cloud services used by employees.

---

## 7. MOBILE THREATS — Target smartphones and tablets.

* **Malicious apps** — Apps containing hidden malware.
* **SIM swapping** — Hijacks phone numbers to bypass MFA.
* **Mobile spyware** — Tracks calls, messages, and location.
* **OS exploits** — Attacks on mobile operating systems.
* **Rogue base stations (IMSI catchers)** — Fake cell towers used for interception.

---

## 8. IoT & OT THREATS — Target smart devices and industrial systems.

* **ICS/SCADA attacks** — Target industrial control systems.
* **Firmware tampering** — Modifies device firmware maliciously.
* **IoT botnets** — Compromises IoT devices for large‑scale attacks.
* **Smart home compromise** — Attacks consumer IoT devices.
* **Critical infrastructure disruption** — Targets utilities and industrial operations.

---

## 9. INSIDER THREATS — Originate from within the organization.

* **Malicious insiders** — Employees intentionally causing harm.
* **Negligent insiders** — Mistakes leading to breaches.
* **Compromised insiders** — Accounts taken over by attackers.

---

## 10. PHYSICAL & HARDWARE THREATS — Require physical access or hardware manipulation.

* **Hardware implants** — Malicious hardware added to systems.
* **Evil‑maid attacks** — Physical tampering when a device is unattended.
* **USB drop attacks** — Malicious USB devices left for victims.
* **RFID cloning** — Copies access badges or cards.

---

## 11. CYBER‑PHYSICAL & CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE THREATS — Cross digital and physical boundaries.

* **Power grid attacks** — Disrupt electrical infrastructure.
* **Water treatment manipulation** — Alters chemical or operational controls.
* **Transportation system compromise** — Targets aviation, rail, or traffic systems.

---

## 12. THREAT ACTOR–DRIVEN THREATS — Defined by who is behind the attack.

* **Nation‑state operations** — Government‑backed espionage or sabotage.
* **Cybercriminal groups** — Financially motivated organized crime.
* **Hacktivists** — Ideologically motivated attackers.
* **Cyber mercenaries** — Contractors offering offensive services.
* **Terrorist cyber units** — Cyberattacks supporting extremist agendas.

---

## 13. EMERGING & ADVANCED THREATS — Cutting‑edge or evolving attack classes.

* **AI‑assisted attacks** — Use AI to automate or enhance attacks.
* **Deepfake social engineering** — Uses synthetic media to deceive.
* **Quantum‑era cryptographic threats** — Break classical encryption.
* **Advanced supply chain attacks** — Compromise trusted software at scale.

---

## 1. AI‑ENABLED ATTACKS — Threats where attackers use AI to enhance or automate attacks

* **AI‑generated phishing** — AI creates highly convincing emails, messages, or scripts that mimic human writing and bypass traditional detection.
* **AI‑powered social engineering** — Attackers use AI to analyze victims’ behavior, speech, and patterns to craft personalized attacks.
* **Deepfake impersonation** — Synthetic audio/video used to impersonate executives, employees, or family members.
* **AI‑driven password cracking** — Machine learning accelerates brute‑force and pattern‑based password guessing.
* **AI‑automated malware generation** — AI creates polymorphic or obfuscated malware that changes constantly to evade detection.
* **AI‑enhanced reconnaissance** — AI scans networks, identifies vulnerabilities, and prioritizes targets faster than humans.
* **AI‑powered botnets** — Botnets that adapt, learn, and optimize attack patterns autonomously.

---

## 2. ATTACKS AGAINST AI SYSTEMS — Threats targeting machine learning models themselves

* **Model poisoning** — Attackers inject malicious data into training sets to corrupt the model’s behavior.
* **Adversarial examples** — Specially crafted inputs that cause AI models to misclassify (e.g., tricking facial recognition).
* **Model inversion** — Extracting sensitive training data from an AI model’s outputs.
* **Model extraction** — Stealing a proprietary AI model by querying it repeatedly.
* **Data poisoning** — Corrupting datasets to degrade model accuracy or introduce backdoors.
* **Prompt injection** — Manipulating AI systems through crafted inputs that override intended behavior.
* **AI supply chain compromise** — Attacking pre‑trained models, libraries, or ML frameworks before deployment.

---

## 3. AI‑ASSISTED MALWARE — Malware that uses AI to adapt, evade, or optimize attacks

* **AI‑adaptive malware** — Malware that learns from defenses and changes behavior in real time.
* **AI‑generated polymorphic malware** — Uses AI to constantly rewrite its code to avoid detection.
* **AI‑driven RATs** — Remote Access Trojans that autonomously escalate privileges or hide activity.
* **AI‑optimized ransomware** — Uses AI to identify high‑value files, backup systems, and fastest encryption paths.

---

## 4. AI MISUSE IN INFORMATION WARFARE — Attacks using AI to manipulate perception or reality

* **Deepfake disinformation** — AI‑generated videos or audio used to spread false information.
* **Synthetic identity fraud** — AI creates realistic fake identities for financial or cybercrime.
* **AI‑generated propaganda** — Automated content used to influence public opinion at scale.
* **Automated influence bots** — AI‑driven social media bots that mimic human behavior.

---

## 5. AI RISKS IN DEFENSIVE SYSTEMS — When AI used for security becomes a vulnerability

* **AI misclassification** — Attackers exploit weaknesses in detection models to bypass security tools.
* **Over‑reliance on AI detection** — Attackers design threats specifically to evade AI‑based defenses.
* **Model drift exploitation** — Attackers take advantage of outdated or degraded AI models.
* **AI logging blind spots** — AI systems may ignore or misinterpret certain attack patterns.

---

## 6. AI THREATS IN CLOUD & ENTERPRISE ENVIRONMENTS

* **Compromised AI APIs** — Attackers exploit AI inference APIs to extract data or models.
* **Unauthorized AI agents** — Rogue AI agents performing actions without proper oversight.
* **Shadow AI** — Employees using unapproved AI tools that expose sensitive data.
* **AI‑driven insider threats** — Insiders using AI to automate data theft or bypass controls.

---

## 7. AI‑RELATED PRIVACY & DATA SECURITY THREATS

* **Training data leakage** — Sensitive data unintentionally exposed through model outputs.
* **Inference attacks** — Attackers deduce private information from model predictions.
* **Dataset reconstruction** — Rebuilding original training data from AI responses.

---

## 8. EMERGING AI THREATS — Cutting‑edge or future‑risk categories

* **Autonomous cyberattacks** — AI systems capable of planning and executing attacks without human input.
* **AI‑powered zero‑day discovery** — AI used to find vulnerabilities faster than defenders can patch.
* **Quantum‑AI hybrid attacks** — Future attacks combining quantum computing and AI to break encryption.
* **AI‑generated synthetic malware ecosystems** — Entire malware families created and evolved by AI.
